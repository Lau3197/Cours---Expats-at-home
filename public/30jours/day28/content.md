Belgium has one of the most accessible citizenship tracks in Europe.
If you plan to stay, this should be your long-term goal.

### The "Short Track" (5 Years)
You can apply for nationality after 5 years if you meet these criteria:
1.  **Residence:** 5 years of legal, continuous residence (registered at the Commune).
2.  **Language:** Proof of **A2 level** in French or Dutch.
    *   *Proof:* A language course certificate, or a Belgian diploma.
3.  **Social Integration:**
    *   A diploma (Bachelor/Master) obtained in Belgium.
    *   OR a specialized "Integration Course" (CitoyennetÃ©) provided by the commune.
    *   OR having worked continuously for 5 years.
4.  **Economic Participation:**
    *   Worked for at least 468 days (full-time equivalent) in the last 5 years.
