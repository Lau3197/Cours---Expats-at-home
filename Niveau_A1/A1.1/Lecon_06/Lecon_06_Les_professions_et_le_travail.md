## French Pronunciation: Key Sounds for This Lesson

**Before we start, here are the key sounds you'll need:**

### The French R [ʁ]

**Examples:**
- **professeur** [pʁɔfɛsœʁ] - teacher
- **travailler** [tʁavaj] - to work
- **médecin** [medsɛ̃] - doctor

### Nasal Sounds

**Examples:**
- **médecin** [medsɛ̃] - the "in" makes a nasal sound [ɛ̃]
- **ingénieur** [ɛ̃ʒenjœʁ] - starts with nasal [ɛ̃]
- **professeur** [pʁɔfɛsœʁ] - has nasal [œ̃]

### The Sound [j] (like "y" in "yes")

**Examples:**
- **travailler** [tʁavaj] - the "ll" sounds like "y"
- **ingénieur** [ɛ̃ʒenjœʁ] - has the sound [j]

## Grammar: Talking About Your Profession

**Remember what you heard in the dialogues?**

- **"Je suis professeur"** (I am a teacher)
- **"Je travaille comme ingénieur"** (I work as an engineer)
- **"Je travaille dans une école"** (I work in a school)

**What did you notice?** In French, you usually DON'T use "un/une" (a/an) with professions!

### How to Say "I am a [profession]"

**Correct:**
- **Je suis professeur.** [ʒə sɥi pʁɔfɛsœʁ] = "I am a teacher."
- **Elle est médecin.** [ɛl ɛ medsɛ̃] = "She is a doctor."
- **Il est ingénieur.** [il ɛ ɛ̃ʒenjœʁ] = "He is an engineer."

**Incorrect:**
- ~~Je suis un professeur.~~ (Don't use "un"!)
- ~~Elle est une médecin.~~ (Don't use "une"!)

**Exception:** Sometimes you CAN use "un/une" if you add more information:
- **Je suis un professeur de français.** [ʒə sɥi œ̃ pʁɔfɛsœʁ də fʁɑ̃sɛ] = "I am a French teacher." (Here it's OK because you're specifying what kind!)

### The Verb "Travailler" (To Work)

**This is how you say "to work" in French!** It's a regular -er verb.

**Conjugation:**

| Pronom | Verbe "travailler" | English | IPA |
|--------|-------------------|---------|-----|
| Je | travaille | I work | [ʒə tʁavaj] |
| Tu | travailles | You work (informal) | [ty tʁavaj] |
| Il/Elle | travaille | He/She works | [il/ɛl tʁavaj] |
| Nous | travaillons | We work | [nu tʁavajɔ̃] |
| Vous | travaillez | You work (formal/plural) | [vu tʁavaje] |
| Ils/Elles | travaillent | They work | [il/ɛl tʁavaj] |

**Examples:**
- **Je travaille à Bruxelles.** [ʒə tʁavaj a bʁyksɛl] = "I work in Brussels."
- **Tu travailles où ?** [ty tʁavaj u] = "Where do you work?"
- **Elle travaille dans une école.** [ɛl tʁavaj dɑ̃z yn ekɔl] = "She works in a school."
- **Nous travaillons ensemble.** [nu tʁavajɔ̃z ɑ̃sɑ̃bl] = "We work together."

**Important:** Notice that "je", "tu", "il", "elle" all use "travaille" - they sound the same but are spelled differently!

### Asking About Someone's Profession

**Formal:**
- **Quelle est votre profession ?** [kɛl ɛ vɔtʁ pʁɔfɛsjɔ̃] = "What is your profession?"
- **Qu'est-ce que vous faites dans la vie ?** [kɛs kə vu fɛt dɑ̃ la vi] = "What do you do for a living?"

**Informal:**
- **Qu'est-ce que tu fais dans la vie ?** [kɛs kə ty fɛ dɑ̃ la vi] = "What do you do for a living?"
- **Tu fais quoi ?** [ty fɛ kwa] = "What do you do?" (very informal)

**How to answer:**
- **Je suis [profession].** [ʒə sɥi] = "I am [profession]."
- **Je travaille comme [profession].** [ʒə tʁavaj kɔm] = "I work as [profession]."
- **Je travaille dans [field].** [ʒə tʁavaj dɑ̃] = "I work in [field]."

**Cultural note:** When introducing yourself professionally in Belgium, always start with "Bonjour"! Example: "Bonjour, je suis [profession]" (not just "Je suis [profession]"). Belgians appreciate professional politeness!

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Watch out for these common mistakes:**

### Mistake 1: Using "un/une" with Professions

**Wrong:** "Je suis un professeur" or "Je suis une médecin"  
**Correct:** "Je suis professeur" or "Je suis médecin"

**Why:** In French, you usually DON'T use "un/une" (a/an) with professions when stating what you are.

### Mistake 2: Wrong Form of "Travailler"

**Wrong:** "Je travaillent" or "Tu travaille"  
**Correct:** "Je travaille" or "Tu travailles"

**Why:** The verb "travailler" must agree with the subject. "Je" uses "travaille", "Tu" uses "travailles".

### Mistake 3: Forgetting Gender Agreement

**Wrong:** "Je suis infirmier" (if you're female)  
**Correct:** "Je suis infirmière" (feminine)

**Why:** Many professions have masculine and feminine forms. Use the form that matches your gender.

### Mistake 4: Not Starting with "Bonjour"

**Wrong:** "Je suis professeur" (without greeting)  
**Correct:** "Bonjour, je suis professeur"

**Why:** In Belgium, it's important to greet people first, especially in professional contexts.

## Belgian Culture: Work Life in Belgium

**Did you know?** Work culture in Belgium has some specific characteristics.

**Working hours:**
- Most people work Monday to Friday, 9 AM to 5 PM
- Lunch break is usually 1 hour (12 PM to 1 PM)
- Many people go home for lunch (especially in smaller cities)

**Work-life balance:**
- Belgians value their free time!
- Vacation: Most people get 20 days of paid vacation per year
- Public holidays: Belgium has 10 public holidays per year

**In Brussels:**
- Many people work for European institutions (European Commission, European Parliament, etc.)
- Multilingualism is important - many jobs require French, Dutch, and English
- International companies are everywhere - you'll meet people from all over the world!

**Tips for Expats:**

When talking about work in Belgium:
1. Be professional but friendly - Belgians are generally friendly but appreciate professionalism
2. Mention languages - If you speak multiple languages, mention it! It's a big plus in Belgium
3. Network! - Brussels has many networking events for expats
4. Learn about Belgian work culture - Understanding the culture helps you integrate better

**Useful phrases for networking:**
- **Enchanté(e) !** [ɑ̃ʃɑ̃te] = Nice to meet you!
- **Ravi(e) de vous rencontrer !** [ʁavi də vu ʁɑ̃kɔ̃tʁe] = Pleased to meet you!
- **À bientôt !** [a bjɛ̃to] = See you soon!
