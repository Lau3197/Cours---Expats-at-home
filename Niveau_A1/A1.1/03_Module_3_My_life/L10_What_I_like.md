## Observation 2: NE...PAS ‚Äî Saying "No" in French!

**Look at the dialogue:**

> je **n'**aime **pas** la pluie
> je **ne** regarde **pas** la t√©l√©

**What do you notice?**

To make a sentence negative, put **NE** before the verb and **PAS** after it:

| Positive | Negative |
|:---------|:---------|
| J'aime le caf√©. | Je **n'**aime **pas** le caf√©. |

### The Rules

1. **NE** goes BEFORE the verb
2. **PAS** goes AFTER the verb
3. **NE** becomes **N'** before a vowel (elision!):
   - Je **ne** parle **pas** ‚Üí correct
   - Je **n'**aime **pas** ‚Üí correct (aime starts with a vowel)
   - Je **n'**habite **pas** ‚Üí correct (habite starts with H)

**In spoken French, the "ne" often disappears:**
- Written: Je **ne** sais **pas**.
- Spoken: "J'sais **pas**."

But as a learner, always use both NE and PAS!
