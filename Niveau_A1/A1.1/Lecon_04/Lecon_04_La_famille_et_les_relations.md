## Listening Comprehension: Talking About Family

**Listen to this conversation between Sophie and Tom:**

**Sophie :** Parle-moi de ta famille !

**Tom :** D'accord ! J'ai un frère et une sœur. Mon frère habite à Londres et ma sœur habite à Paris. Mes parents habitent en Angleterre.

**Comprehension Questions:**

1. How many brothers and sisters does Tom have?

3. Does Tom have children?
4. How many children does Sophie have?

**Answers:**
1. One brother and one sister
2. In England
3. No, not yet
4. Two children (one son and one daughter)

## Grammar: Possessive Adjectives (Mon, Ma, Mes)

**Remember what you heard in the dialogue?**

- **Mon frère** habite à Londres (My brother lives in London)
- **Ma sœur** habite à Paris (My sister lives in Paris)
- **Mes parents** habitent en Angleterre (My parents live in England)

**What did you notice?** French uses different words for "my" depending on the gender and number!

### How Possessive Adjectives Work

**The possessive adjective agrees with the object (the thing you own), not with you!**

**For "my" (mon, ma, mes):**

| Masculine singular | Feminine singular | Plural (both) |
|-------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| mon | ma | mes |
| mon père | ma mère | mes parents |
| mon frère | ma sœur | mes enfants |
| mon fils | ma fille | mes grands-parents |

**Examples:**
- **Mon père** habite à Bruxelles. (My father lives in Brussels.)
- **Ma mère** est belge. (My mother is Belgian.)
- **Mes parents** habitent en Angleterre. (My parents live in England.)

### Important Rule: "Mon" Before Vowels

**When a feminine noun starts with a vowel, use "mon" instead of "ma":**

- **Mon amie** (my friend - feminine) - Correct
- ~~Ma amie~~ - Incorrect (sounds wrong - two vowels together!)

**Why?** It's easier to pronounce "mon amie" than "ma amie".

### Complete Table: All Possessive Adjectives

| Person | Masculine singular | Feminine singular | Plural |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|--------|
| Je (I) | mon | ma | mes |
| Tu (You - informal) | ton | ta | tes |
| Il/Elle (He/She) | son | sa | ses |
| Nous (We) | notre | notre | nos |
| Vous (You - formal/plural) | votre | votre | vos |
| Ils/Elles (They) | leur | leur | leurs |

**For now, focus on "mon, ma, mes" (my) - you'll use these the most!**

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Watch out for these common mistakes:**

### Mistake 1: "Ma amie" Instead of "Mon amie"

**Wrong:** "Ma amie habite à Paris"  
**Correct:** "Mon amie habite à Paris"

**Why:** Before a vowel, use "mon" even with feminine nouns. "Ma amie" sounds wrong - it becomes "mon amie".

### Mistake 2: "Mon parents" Instead of "Mes parents"

**Wrong:** "Mon parents habitent en Espagne"  
**Correct:** "Mes parents habitent en Espagne"

**Why:** "Parents" is plural, so you need "mes" (my plural), not "mon" (my singular).

### Mistake 3: "Mon mère" Instead of "Ma mère"

**Wrong:** "Mon mère est belge"  
**Correct:** "Ma mère est belge"

**Why:** "Mère" is feminine, so use "ma" (not "mon" which is for masculine). Remember: mon/ton/son for masculine, ma/ta/sa for feminine.

### Mistake 4: Confusing "son/sa/ses" (his/her)

**Wrong:** "Son mère" or "Sa père"  
**Correct:** "Son père" (his/her father - masculine), "Sa mère" (his/her mother - feminine)

**Why:** "Son/sa/ses" agree with the object, not the person. "Son" for masculine nouns, "sa" for feminine nouns.

## Belgian Culture: Family in Belgium

**Did you know?** In Belgium, family is very important. Many Belgians live near their parents and grandparents. Family meals, especially on Sunday, are an important tradition.
