## Mastering Mobility in Brussels

Getting around Brussels is easy once you understand the acronyms: STIB, SNCB, De Lijn, TEC.

### The Golden Ticket: MOBIB Card
The MOBIB card is a unified contactless card that works for all Belgian transport operators.
*   **Cost:** €6 for the card itself (valid 5 years).
*   **Where to get it:** At a "Kiosk" or "Bootik" in major metro stations (Porte de Namur, Midi, Rogers, Schuman). Bring your ID and a photo.

### Subscriptions (Abonnements)
1.  **STIB Standard Season Ticket:**
    *   **Price:** ~€55 / month (or ~€600 / year).
    *   **Coverage:** All STIB Metro, Tram, and Bus lines within Brussels.
2.  **Brupass XL:**
    *   **Price:** Slightly higher (~€84 / month).
    *   **Coverage:** Includes STIB *PLUS* trains (SNCB) and regional buses (De Lijn, TEC) in an extended zone around Brussels (~11.5km radius). **Highway recommended for commuters living in outskirts.**
3.  **Student / Young Person:**
    *   If you are 18-24 and Brussels resident: **€12 per year.** Yes, really.

### The Operators
*   **STIB / MIVB:** Metro, Tram, Bus inside Brussels. (Blue/Red branding)
*   **SNCB / NMBS:** National Trains. Creating great cross-city shortcuts (e.g., Midi to North Station in 4 mins).
*   **De Lijn:** Buses from Flanders (Yellow/White).
*   **TEC:** Buses from Wallonia (Yellow/Red).

### Cycling & Scooters
*   **Villo!:** City bike-sharing scheme. Durable, heavy yellow bikes. Stations everywhere. Cheap yearly subscription.
*   **Floating Scooters:** Bolt, Lime, Dott. Everywhere. Require their own apps.
*   **Rules:** Helmets are not mandatory but recommended. **Priority to the Right** applies on roads unless signed otherwise!
