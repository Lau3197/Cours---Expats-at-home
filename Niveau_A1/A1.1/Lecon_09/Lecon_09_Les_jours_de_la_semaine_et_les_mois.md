## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Before we dive into grammar, watch out for these common mistakes:**

**Mistake 1:** Capitalizing days and months âŒ  
**Correct:** "lundi" (not "Lundi"), "janvier" (not "Janvier") âœ…  
**Why:** In French, days of the week and months are NOT capitalized (unlike English). Only capitalize them if they're at the start of a sentence.

**Mistake 2:** "C'est 15 mars" (missing "le") âŒ  
**Correct:** "C'est le 15 mars" âœ…  
**Why:** Always use "le" before dates in French. "C'est le 15" not "C'est 15".

**Mistake 3:** "Nous sommes en lundi" (wrong preposition) âŒ  
**Correct:** "Nous sommes lundi" or "C'est lundi" âœ…  
**Why:** For days, you don't need a preposition. Just say "Nous sommes lundi" or "On est lundi".

**Mistake 4:** Confusing "en janvier" and "le janvier" âŒ  
**Correct:** "en janvier" (in January), "le 15 janvier" (January 15th) âœ…  
**Why:** Use "en" for months alone (en janvier), but "le" for specific dates (le 15 janvier).

**Mistake 5:** Saying "1 janvier" instead of "1er janvier" âŒ  
**Correct:** "le 1er janvier" (le premier janvier) âœ…  
**Why:** For the 1st of the month, use "1er" (premier = first) not just "1". For other days, use regular numbers (2, 3, 4...).
