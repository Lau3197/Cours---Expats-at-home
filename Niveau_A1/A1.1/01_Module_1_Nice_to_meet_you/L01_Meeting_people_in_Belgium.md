## Let's Start! First Meeting

### Listen to this dialogue

**Tom has just arrived in Brussels. He meets Sophie at a café:**

> **Sophie :** Bonjour !
>
> **Tom :** Bonjour !
>
> **Sophie :** **Je suis** Sophie. Et vous ?
>
> **Tom :** **Je suis** Tom. Je suis américain.
>
> **Sophie :** Ah, moi je suis belge. Vous êtes à Bruxelles pour le travail ?
>
> **Tom :** Oui, je suis ingénieur et j'habite à Ixelles.
>
> **Sophie :** Ah, moi aussi ! Enchantée, Tom !
>
> **Tom :** Enchanté, Sophie ! À tantôt !
>
> **Sophie :** À tantôt !

<details>
<summary>English Translation</summary>

> **Sophie:** Hello!
>
> **Tom:** Hello!
>
> **Sophie:** My name is Sophie. And you?
>
> **Tom:** My name is Tom. I am American.
>
> **Sophie:** Ah, I am Belgian. Are you in Brussels for work?
>
> **Tom:** Yes, I am an engineer and I live in Ixelles.
>
> **Sophie:** Oh, me too! Nice to meet you, Tom!
>
> **Tom:** Nice to meet you, Sophie! See you later!
>
> **Sophie:** See you later!

</details>

## Observation 1: Greetings — Saying Hello and Goodbye

**Look at the beginning and end of the dialogue:**

> **Sophie :** **Bonjour** !
> **Tom :** **Bonjour** !
> (...)
> **Tom :** **À tantôt** !
> **Sophie :** **À tantôt** !

**What do you notice?**

- Both people say "**Bonjour**" when they meet
- They say "**À tantôt**" when they leave — this is a Belgian way of saying "See you later!"

### Essential Greetings

| French | English | When? |
|:-------|:--------|:------|
| Bonjour | Hello / Good morning | All day until evening |
| Bonsoir | Good evening | After 6pm |
| Au revoir | Goodbye | When leaving (any time) |
| **À tantôt** | See you later | **Belgian expression!** Very common in Belgium |
| Bonne journée | Have a nice day | When leaving (daytime) |
| Bonne soirée | Have a nice evening | When leaving (evening) |
| Salut | Hi / Bye | Informal, with friends only |

**Important in Belgium:** You MUST say "Bonjour" or "Bonsoir" when entering a shop, a bakery, a restaurant. It is considered rude not to.

### Essential Politeness

| French | English |
|:-------|:--------|
| S'il vous plaît | Please (formal) |
| Merci | Thank you |
| Merci beaucoup | Thank you very much |
| Excusez-moi | Excuse me |
| Pardon | Sorry / Excuse me |
| Je vous en prie | You're welcome (formal) |
| Avec plaisir | With pleasure (Belgian way to say "you're welcome") |

## Observation 2: Saying Your Name — "Je suis"

**Look at the dialogue again:**

> **Sophie :** **Je suis** Sophie. Et vous ?
>
> **Tom :** **Je suis** Tom.

**What do you notice?**

- To say your name: **Je suis** + [your name]
- "Je suis" = "I am" (you already know this from ÊTRE!)
- "Et vous ?" = "And you?" (formal)

> **Good to know:**
>
> You will also hear French speakers say **"Je m'appelle..."** (My name is...). This uses a different verb form that you will learn later. For now, **"Je suis + name"** is all you need!

## Observation 3: The Verb ÊTRE (to be) — The Most Important Verb!

**Look at the dialogue again:**

> **Je suis** américain. (I am American)
> **Je suis** belge. (I am Belgian)
> **Je suis** ingénieur. (I am an engineer)
> **Vous êtes** à Bruxelles ? (Are you in Brussels?)

**What do you notice?**

- "Je suis" = "I am"
- "Vous êtes" = "You are" (formal)
- We use ÊTRE to say who we are: name, nationality, job

### Full Conjugation of ÊTRE

| French | Pronunciation | English |
|:-------|:-------------|:--------|
| je **suis** | /ʒə sɥi/ | I am |
| tu **es** | /ty ɛ/ | you are (informal) |
| il **est** | /il ɛ/ | he is |
| elle **est** | /ɛl ɛ/ | she is |
| nous **sommes** | /nu sɔm/ | we are |
| vous **êtes** | /vu zɛt/ | you are (formal / plural) |
| ils **sont** | /il sɔ̃/ | they are (masculine) |
| elles **sont** | /ɛl sɔ̃/ | they are (feminine) |

**Important — Elision:**
When "je" is before a vowel sound, it becomes **j'** :
- Je habite → **J'habite** (I live)
- Je ai → **J'ai** (I have — you'll learn this in the next lesson!)

This is called **elision**. You will see it everywhere in French.

## Observation 4: Tu or Vous? — A Belgian Essential

**Look at the dialogue:**

> **Sophie :** Et **vous** ? **Vous êtes** à Bruxelles pour le travail ?

**Why "vous" and not "tu"?**

Sophie and Tom just met. In Belgium, you ALWAYS use **vous** with:

| Use VOUS with: | Use TU with: |
|:----------------|:-------------|
| Strangers | Close friends |
| Shopkeepers, waiters | Family members |
| Colleagues (at first) | Children |
| Anyone you don't know well | People who say "On se tutoie ?" |

**In Belgium, people use VOUS more than in France.** When in doubt, always use VOUS. The other person will say "On peut se tutoyer" (We can use tu) if they want to switch.

### ÊTRE with Tu vs Vous

| Informal (tu) | Formal (vous) |
|:--------------|:-------------|
| Tu **es** belge ? | Vous **êtes** belge ? |
| Tu **es** d'où ? | Vous **êtes** d'où ? |

## Observation 5: Nationalities — Masculine and Feminine

**Look at these sentences:**

> Tom : "Je suis **américain**." (Tom is a man)
> Sophie : "Je suis **belge**." (Sophie is a woman)

### The Rule: Adding -e for Feminine

For many nationalities, you add **-e** for the feminine form:

| Masculine (man) | Feminine (woman) | English |
|:----------------|:-----------------|:--------|
| français | français**e** | French |
| américain | américain**e** | American |
| allemand | allemand**e** | German |
| espagnol | espagnol**e** | Spanish |
| anglais | anglais**e** | English |
| japonais | japonais**e** | Japanese |

**Some nationalities don't change:**

| Same for M and F | English |
|:-----------------|:--------|
| belge | Belgian |
| suisse | Swiss |
| russe | Russian |

**Some have special forms:**

| Masculine | Feminine | English |
|:----------|:---------|:--------|
| italien | italien**ne** | Italian |
| brésilien | brésilien**ne** | Brazilian |
| turc | tur**que** | Turkish |

### Connectors: ET and OU

To link ideas, use:
- **et** = and → "Je suis américain **et** je suis ingénieur."
- **ou** = or → "Tu es belge **ou** français ?"

## Culture: Greetings in Belgium

### What You Need to Know

**La bise (cheek kiss)**

In Belgium, people often do **one** cheek kiss when greeting friends and family. This is different from France where they do 2, 3, or even 4 depending on the region!

- **1 kiss** in most of Belgium
- Start with the **right cheek**
- Only with people you know — NOT with strangers
- In professional settings: **handshake**, not la bise

**Marc, 42, Brussels:**

> **En français :**
> "En Belgique, on dit toujours 'Bonjour' quand on entre dans un magasin ou un café. C'est très important ! Et quand on part, on dit 'Au revoir' ou 'À tantôt'. C'est la base de la politesse belge."

> **In English:**
> "In Belgium, we always say 'Bonjour' when entering a store or a café. It's very important! And when leaving, we say 'Au revoir' or 'À tantôt'. It's the basis of Belgian politeness."

### The Belgian Vouvoiement

Belgians use "vous" MORE than the French. Even in casual settings, many Belgians keep using "vous" with colleagues for years. When in doubt: **always vous**.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Forgetting "Bonjour"

| Wrong | Correct |
|:------|:--------|
| "Où est la gare ?" | "**Bonjour**, où est la gare ?" |

In Belgium, ALWAYS start with "Bonjour"!

### Mistake 2: Using "tu" too quickly

| Wrong | Correct |
|:------|:--------|
| "Tu es le docteur ?" (to a doctor you just met) | "**Vous êtes** le docteur ?" |

In Belgium, use VOUS with anyone you don't know well.

### Mistake 3: Forgetting elision

| Wrong | Correct |
|:------|:--------|
| "Je habite à Bruxelles" | "**J'habite** à Bruxelles" |
| "Je ai un chat" | "**J'ai** un chat" |

When "je" comes before a vowel (a, e, i, o, u), it becomes **j'**.

## Grammar Summary

### The Verb ÊTRE (to be)

| Subject | Conjugation | Example |
|:--------|:------------|:--------|
| je | suis | Je suis belge. |
| tu | es | Tu es américain ? |
| il/elle | est | Elle est professeure. |
| nous | sommes | Nous sommes étudiants. |
| vous | êtes | Vous êtes à Bruxelles ? |
| ils/elles | sont | Ils sont ingénieurs. |

### Uses of ÊTRE

| For... | Example |
|:-------|:--------|
| Name | Je suis Tom. |
| Nationality | Je suis française. |
| Profession | Je suis ingénieur. |
| Location | Je suis à Bruxelles. |

### Elision

When **je** comes before a vowel → **j'**
- j'habite, j'ai, j'aime

When **le/la** comes before a vowel → **l'**
- l'homme, l'école, l'ami

### Connectors

- **et** = and → "Je suis belge **et** professeur."
- **ou** = or → "Vous êtes français **ou** belge ?"
