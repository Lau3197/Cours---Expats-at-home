## 03:00 - Observation 1: Greetings
*(Correspondance : "Observation 1: Greetings" & "Practice 1")*

**(Slide : "Observation 1: Greetings")**

**(Vous)**
Look at how they started. They both said **"Bonjour"**.

I want you to repeat this word. It is the absolute foundation of French politeness.
*Bon-jour*. (Literaturely: Good Day).

We use it in the morning and afternoon.
If it were evening (usually after 6 PM), they would have said **"Bonsoir"**.

*(Petit geste vers le bas)*
Below this video, you have a "Practice 1" section where you can test yourself on when to use which greeting. But for now, just remember: Day = Bonjour. Evening = Bonsoir.

## 07:00 - Observation 3: Saying your nationality
*(Correspondance : "Observation 3: Saying your nationality" & "Practice 2")*

**(Slide : "Observation 3")**

**(Vous)**
Now, let's look at nationalities.
> **Tom :** Je suis américain.
> **Sophie :** Je suis belge.

We use the **same structure**: "Je suis".

**BUT**... French has genders. This is "Observation 3".
If you are a man, you use the masculine form. If you are a woman, you often add an **-e** at the end.

Listen to the difference:
*   (Masculine) Américain
*   (Feminine) Américain**e** (You hear the N!)

*   (Masculine) Français
*   (Feminine) Français**e** (You hear the Z sound!)

Check the vocabulary list below specifically for your nationality later.

## 10:00 - Observation 4: Saying where you live
*(Correspondance : "Observation 4: Saying where you live")*

**(Slide : "Observation 4")**

**(Vous)**
Moving on. Sophie asks: "Vous habitez où?" (Where do you live?).
Tom answers:

> **Tom :** J'habite **à** Bruxelles.

He didn't use "Je suis" here. He used **"J'habite"**, from the verb "Habiter" (To live).

And notice the little word **"à"**.
In English, you say "I live **in** Brussels".
In French, for cities, we say "J'habite **à** Bruxelles", "J'habite **à** Paris".

Don't forget the "à"!

## 13:30 - Culture: Greetings in Belgium
*(Correspondance : "Culture: Greetings in Belgium")*

**(Slide : Drapeau Belge / "Culture")**
**(Face Caméra - Ton sérieux et confidentiel)**

Now, before we move to grammar, I need to tell you something crucial about Belgium.
In the lesson below, you'll see a section on "Culture".

Here is the golden rule: **Always say "Bonjour".**

In some countries, you can walk into a shop silently. In Belgium, if you enter a bakery or an elevator without a loud, clear "Bonjour", people will think you are rude. It's almost aggressive to be silent!
So, whenever you open a door to a public place: Smile and say "Bonjour!". It's your magic key.

## 15:00 - Grammar: The verb ÊTRE
*(Correspondance : "Grammar: The verb ÊTRE (to be)")*

**(Slide : Tableau de conjugaison "Être")**

**(Vous)**
Let's quickly formalize what we used today. We mostly used the verb **Être** (To Be).

*   **Je suis** (I am) - Used for name, nationality, profession.
*   **Vous êtes** (You are) - Used by Sophie to ask "Vous êtes belge ?" (Are you Belgian?).

You will find the full conjugation table in the Grammar section below. Don't try to memorize the whole table today, just focus on "Je suis" and "Vous êtes".

## 16:30 - Common Mistakes to Avoid
*(Correspondance : "Common Mistakes to Avoid")*

**(Slide : "Common Mistakes - ⚠️")**

**(Vous)**
I want to save you from three classic mistakes beginners make.

1.  **The "Bonjour" mistake:** We just talked about it. Don't be the silent tourist!
2.  **The "Je suis" vs "J'habite" mistake:**
    *   Say "Je suis américain" (Identity).
    *   Say "J'habite à Bruxelles" (Location).
    *   Don't say "Je suis à Bruxelles" to mean you live there (that just means you contain yourself inside the city right now!).
3.  **The missing "à":** It's "J'habite **à** Bruxelles", not "J'habite Bruxelles".
