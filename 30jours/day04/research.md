# Brussels Rental Contracts in 2026

Brussels rental contracts are now governed by strict regulations designed to combat excessive rents and protect tenant rights. Here's what you need to know:

## Rent Price Controls

Rents are considered **excessive** if they exceed the reference rent (median rent for the property) by more than 20%.[1] The reference rent is published on huurprijzen.brussels / loyers.brussels and updated annually.[1] However, rents exceeding this 20% threshold may be justified if the property has substantial comfort features, or rents below the threshold may still be excessive if the property has quality defects.[1]

If you believe your rent is excessive, you can request a free, non-binding opinion from the joint rental commission within the first 2-3 months of your lease (depending on contract duration).[1] If disputes arise, courts can issue binding decisions and may retroactively adjust rents up to 4 months prior.[1]

## Pre-Signing Information Requirements

Before signing, your landlord must provide:[3]

- Property description
- Rent amount and reference rent
- Meter information (water, gas, electricity)
- List and estimate of charges
- Energy certificate (EPB)
- Property management company details

For short-term leases (3 years or less) renewed or entered into on or after November 1, 2024, landlords must additionally disclose the lease type, the last rent amount paid, and the date it was paid.[2][3]

## Mandatory Contract Annexes

Landlords must provide a **22-page explanatory appendix** outlining Brussels rental rules, including safety standards, tenant rights, and lease registration procedures.[2][4]

## Registration Requirements

Since January 1, 2025, Brussels implemented a **dual registration system**: leases must be registered with both federal and Brussels government authorities.[2][5] Registration is free and compulsory, and landlords must complete it within 2 months of signing.[3] If the landlord fails to register, you can terminate the contract without notice or compensation.[3]

## Rent Revisions

Parties may agree to revise rent between the 9th and 6th month before the end of each three-year period.[1] You can request a commission opinion on whether a revised rent is reasonable.[1]

## Late Payment Terms

If applicable, annual interest for late payment is now 4.5% (the legal rate), and landlords cannot charge additional administration fees for payment reminders.[2]

## Key Rules for 2026

**Payment and deposit restrictions:**
- Cash payments are no longer permitted.[1]
- Personal deposits in addition to the rental guarantee are prohibited, except for student leases.[1]
- The deposit must be held in a blocked account at a bank.[3][8]

**Deposit return timeline:**
- Landlords must return the deposit within two months after the tenant returns the keys.[1]
- If landlords fail to meet this deadline, they incur a penalty of 10% of monthly rent for each month overdue.[1]

**Account setup:**
- You don't need to be a customer of a specific bank to open a rental guarantee account—you only need a valid Belgian ID card.[8]
- Most major Belgian banks (KBC Brussels, BNP Paribas Fortis, ING) offer free blocked accounts for this purpose.[3][8]
- The capital and any interest generated remain blocked for the duration of your lease and are unblocked when you move out and vacate the property.[3]

## Additional Context

These rules are part of Brussels' updated Housing Code (lease ordinance) approved by the Brussels Parliament in April 2024. The changes aim to provide greater security for tenants while establishing clearer frameworks for landlords. The €2-month cap applies to both private and public landlords in the Brussels Region.
