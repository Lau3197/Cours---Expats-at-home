## Let's Start! Apartment Hunting

### Listen to this dialogue

**Tom is looking for a new apartment. He calls about an ad:**

> **Tom :** Bonjour ! J'appelle pour l'appartement à louer à Ixelles.
>
> **Propriétaire :** Bonjour ! Oui, c'est un appartement de **deux** chambres.
>
> **Tom :** C'est combien le loyer ?
>
> **Propriétaire :** Le loyer est de **huit cent cinquante** euros par mois, charges comprises.
>
> **Tom :** Et la garantie locative ?
>
> **Propriétaire :** C'est **deux mois** de loyer, donc **mille sept cents** euros.
>
> **Tom :** D'accord. L'appartement a un salon ?
>
> **Propriétaire :** Oui ! Il y a un grand salon, une cuisine équipée, **deux** chambres, une salle de bains et un balcon.
>
> **Tom :** Super ! C'est au quel étage ?
>
> **Propriétaire :** Au **troisième** étage. L'adresse, c'est **deux cent quarante-cinq**, rue de la Paix.
>
> **Tom :** Parfait ! Je peux visiter quand ?

<details>
<summary>English Translation</summary>

> **Tom:** Hello! I'm calling about the apartment for rent in Ixelles.
>
> **Landlord:** Hello! Yes, it's a **two**-bedroom apartment.
>
> **Tom:** How much is the rent?
>
> **Landlord:** The rent is **eight hundred fifty** euros per month, charges included.
>
> **Tom:** And the security deposit?
>
> **Landlord:** It's **two months'** rent, so **one thousand seven hundred** euros.
>
> **Tom:** OK. Does the apartment have a living room?
>
> **Landlord:** Yes! There's a big living room, a fitted kitchen, **two** bedrooms, a bathroom, and a balcony.
>
> **Tom:** Great! What floor is it on?
>
> **Landlord:** The **third** floor. The address is **two hundred forty-five** Rue de la Paix.
>
> **Tom:** Perfect! When can I visit?

</details>

---

### Comprehension

1. How many bedrooms does the apartment have?
2. How much is the rent per month?
3. How much is the security deposit?
4. What floor is it on?
5. What is the address?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **Two** bedrooms — 2. **850€** per month — 3. **1,700€** (two months) — 4. **Third** floor — 5. **245**, rue de la Paix

</details>

## Observation 1: Numbers 100-1000+

### Hundreds

| Number | French |
|:-------|:-------|
| 100 | cent |
| 200 | deux cents |
| 300 | trois cents |
| 400 | quatre cents |
| 500 | cinq cents |
| 600 | six cents |
| 700 | sept cents |
| 800 | huit cents |
| 900 | neuf cents |

**Rule:** "cent" gets an **-s** when it's a round hundred (200 = deux cent**s**), but NO -s when followed by another number (201 = deux cent un).

### Combining: 101, 245, 850...

| Number | French |
|:-------|:-------|
| 101 | cent un |
| 150 | cent cinquante |
| 245 | deux cent quarante-cinq |
| 500 | cinq cents |
| 750 | sept cent cinquante |
| 850 | huit cent cinquante |

### Thousands

| Number | French |
|:-------|:-------|
| 1 000 | mille |
| 1 500 | mille cinq cents |
| 1 700 | mille sept cents |
| 2 000 | deux mille |
| 5 000 | cinq mille |
| 10 000 | dix mille |

**Rule:** "mille" NEVER takes an -s. Always "mille", never "milles".

## Culture: Housing in Belgium

### The Belgian Lease (Le Bail)

- **Standard lease:** 3-6-9 years (you can leave every 3 years)
- **Security deposit (garantie locative):** Usually 2-3 months of rent
- **Charges:** Heating, water, building maintenance (sometimes included)
- **Registration:** You MUST register at the commune when you move!

### Typical Rents in Brussels (2024)

| Area | Studio | 1-bed | 2-bed |
|:-----|:-------|:------|:------|
| Centre | 700-900€ | 850-1100€ | 1100-1500€ |
| Ixelles/Saint-Gilles | 650-850€ | 800-1050€ | 1000-1400€ |
| Schaerbeek/Etterbeek | 550-750€ | 700-950€ | 900-1200€ |
