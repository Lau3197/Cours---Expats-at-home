Food quality in Belgium is generally very high. But knowing *where* to shop can save you hundreds of euros a month.

### The Major Supermarkets
1.  **Colruyt:**
    *   *Vibe:* Warehouse, industrial, bulk buying.
    *   *Pros:* Cheapest prices (they match competitors).
    *   *Quirk:* No conveyor belts. The staff moves items from your full cart to an empty cart at lightning speed.
2.  **Delhaize:**
    *   *Vibe:* Standard to Upscale.
    *   *Pros:* Excellent fresh produce, great wine selection, "local" focus.
    *   *Cons:* More expensive.
3.  **Carrefour (Hyper / Market / Express):**
    *   *Express* shops are everywhere and open Sundays/late, but are VERY expensive. Use only for emergencies.
4.  **Aldi / Lidl:**
    *   *Vibe:* Discounters.
    *   *Pros:* Great value staples, surprisingly good chocolate and pastries.

### Recycling & Trash (Strict!)
Brussels is strict on recycling.
*   **Yellow Bags:** Paper / Cardboard.
*   **Blue Bags:** Plastic / Cans / Cartons (PMC).
*   **White Bags:** General residual waste.
*   **Green/Orange Bags:** Organic / Food waste (Mandatory in many specific communes now).
