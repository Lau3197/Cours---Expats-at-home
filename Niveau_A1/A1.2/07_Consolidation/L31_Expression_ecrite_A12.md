# Expression Écrite — A1.2 Final

**Level**: A1.2 — Final Consolidation  
**Estimated duration**: 2-3 hours  
**Goal**: Demonstrate all writing skills acquired during A1.2

---

## Instructions

This is a comprehensive writing assessment covering all the grammar, vocabulary, and cultural knowledge from A1.2. Complete all 7 tasks.

---

## Task 1: An Email to a Friend (50-70 words)

**Write an email to a friend describing what you did last weekend in Belgium.**

**Must include:**
- Passé composé (at least 4 verbs, mixing AVOIR and ÊTRE)
- Time markers (samedi, dimanche, hier...)
- At least 1 negative sentence
- A Belgian activity or place

> **De :** [votre email]  
> **À :** ami@email.com  
> **Objet :** Mon week-end en Belgique !
>
> Salut [prénom] !
>
> [Votre texte ici...]
>
> À bientôt !  
> [Votre prénom]

---

## Task 2: Your Daily Routine (60-80 words)

**Describe a typical day in your life in Belgium.**

**Must include:**
- Reflexive verbs (at least 3)
- Telling time (at least 3 times)
- -IR verbs (at least 1)
- DEVOIR or IL FAUT (at least 1)
- Connectors (d'abord, ensuite, puis, après, enfin)

---

## Task 3: A Housing Ad + Inquiry (40 words + 8-line dialogue)

### Part A: Write a housing ad
Create an ad for an apartment in a Belgian city.
**Include:** type, rooms, floor, price, charges, description (2-3 adjectives)

### Part B: Write a phone inquiry dialogue (8 lines)
Call about the ad you wrote. Ask at least 3 questions.

---

## Task 4: Making Plans (8-10 lines of dialogue)

**Write a dialogue where you invite a friend to a Belgian event or place.**

**Must include:**
- VOULOIR and POUVOIR (at least 1 each)
- A suggestion (on pourrait, si on allait, ça te dit de)
- An acceptance and a refusal (for different activities)
- Practical details (time, place, transport)
- Futur proche (at least 2)

---

## Task 5: My Belgian Guide (80-100 words)

**Write a mini-guide for someone coming to live in Belgium.**

**Must include:**
- Practical advice (commune, mutuelle, housing)
- Food recommendations
- A comparison with another country (plus... que, meilleur... que)
- An opinion (je pense que, à mon avis)
- Weather information
- At least 1 belgicisme (septante, à tantôt, dîner/souper...)

---

## Task 6: A Belgian Recipe (40-60 words)

**Write a simple recipe for a Belgian dish (gaufres, carbonade, croque-monsieur...).**

**Must include:**
- The imperative (at least 4 verbs: coupez, ajoutez, mélangez, mettez...)
- Partitive articles (du beurre, de la farine, des œufs...)
- Quantities (un kilo de, une cuillère de, un peu de...)
- PRENDRE or BOIRE (at least 1)
- A suggestion for a drink to serve with it

**Example start:**
> Pour les gaufres de Liège, prenez de la farine, du sucre, du beurre et des œufs...

---

## Task 7: A Day at the Market — Dialogue (8-10 lines)

**Write a dialogue at a Brussels market. You buy fruit, vegetables, and cheese.**

**Must include:**
- Partitive articles (du fromage, de la salade, des pommes...)
- PRENDRE and BOIRE
- Prices and quantities (un kilo de, une barquette de...)
- Telling time (at least 2 times)
- A belgicisme (septante, nonante, une fois...)

## Grading Criteria

Each task is evaluated on:

| Criterion | Description |

Before submitting, verify you've used:

- [ ] Negation in passé composé
- [ ] Reflexive verbs (se lever, se coucher, se préparer...)

- [ ] DEVOIR / IL FAUT
- [ ] Futur proche (ALLER + infinitive)
- [ ] VOULOIR / POUVOIR

- [ ] Opinions (je pense que, je trouve que)
- [ ] Weather expressions (il fait beau, il pleut...)
- [ ] FAIRE DU / JOUER AU
- [ ] Formal register (VOUS) where appropriate
- [ ] Imperative (coupez, mélangez, ajoutez...)
- [ ] Partitive articles (du, de la, de l', des)
- [ ] PRENDRE / BOIRE
- [ ] Telling time (il est 8 heures, à midi, à 14h30...)
