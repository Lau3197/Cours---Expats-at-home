## Troubleshooting: Having Trouble?

**If you're struggling with "aimer" conjugation:**
- Write out the conjugation 5 times: j'aime, tu aimes, il/elle aime, nous aimons, vous aimez, ils/elles aiment
- Practice "J'aime" 20 times - make it automatic
- Remember: "J'aime" (I like), "Tu aimes" (You like), "Il/Elle aime" (He/She likes)
- Pay attention to the endings: -e, -es, -e, -ons, -ez, -ent

**If negative sentences confuse you:**
- Remember: "Je n'aime pas" needs both "ne" and "pas"
- Practice pattern: "Je n'aime pas [thing]" - repeat it 20 times
- Common mistake: forgetting "pas" - always include it!
- Practice: "J'aime le chocolat" â†’ "Je n'aime pas le chocolat"

**If using articles with "aimer" is confusing:**
- Remember: "J'aime le chocolat" (I like chocolate in general)
- Practice: "J'aime le/la/les [thing]" - always use the article
- Exception: with infinitives, no article: "J'aime manger" (not "J'aime le manger")

**If "j'aime" vs "j'adore" confuses you:**
- "J'aime" = I like (normal preference)
- "J'adore" = I love (stronger preference)
- "Je n'aime pas" = I don't like (negative)
- "Je dÃ©teste" = I hate (very negative)
- Start with "J'aime" and "Je n'aime pas" - add others gradually

**Still stuck?** "Aimer" is a very common verb. Focus on mastering "J'aime" and "Je n'aime pas" first. Practice daily with things you actually like/dislike - it helps!
