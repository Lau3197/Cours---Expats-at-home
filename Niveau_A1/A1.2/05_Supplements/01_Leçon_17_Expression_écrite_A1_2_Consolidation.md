## �S�️ Practice 1: Ma journée typique (My typical day)

**Write a short text (60-80 words) describing your daily routine.**

**Include:**
- What time you wake up/go to bed
- Your morning routine (se lever, se laver, prendre le petit-déjeuner)
- Your activities during the day
- What you do in the evening

**Useful verbs:**
- Je me lève à...
- Je prends le petit-déjeuner à...
- Je travaille de... à...
- Le soir, je...
- Je me couche à...

**Example start:**
> "Le matin, je me lève à 7h30. Je me lave et je m'habille. Ensuite, je prends mon petit-déjeuner..."

## �S�️ Practice 2: Mon emploi du temps (My schedule)

**Write your weekly schedule (70-90 words).**

**Include:**
- What you do each day of the week
- Your work/study hours
- Your leisure activities
- When you do groceries or chores

**Useful expressions:**
- Le lundi, je...
- Du mardi au jeudi, je...
- Le week-end, je...
- Tous les jours, je...
- Deux fois par semaine, je...

**Structure:**
1. Introduction: "Ma semaine est assez..."
2. Working days activities
3. Weekend activities
4. Conclusion

## �x� Belgian Context: Writing Tips

**When writing in a Belgian context:**

- **Time format**: Use 24h format in formal writing (14h30, not 2:30 PM)
- **Politeness**: Always include "s'il te plaît" or "s'il vous plaît"
- **Formality**: 
  - Friends: "Salut", "ì plus", "Bisous"
  - Formal: "Bonjour", "Cordialement", "Bien à vous"
- **Numbers**: Remember 70 = septante, 90 = nonante
- **Common Belgian expressions**:
  - "Chouette!" (Nice!/Cool!)
  - "Un pain" (a loaf of bread, not "du pain")
  - "Tantôt" (later/in a bit)
