## Observation 1: LE / LA / LES — "THE" in French

**Look at the dialogue:**

> **le** pain au chocolat → the chocolate croissant
> **la** baguette → the baguette
> **les** croissants → the croissants

**What do you notice?**

In English, "the" is always "the." In French, there are THREE forms!

### The Rule

| | Singular | Plural |
|:--|:---------|:-------|
| **Masculine** | **le** | **les** |
| **Feminine** | **la** | **les** |
| **Before a vowel** | **l'** | **les** |

### Examples

| Article | Example | English |
|:--------|:--------|:--------|
| **le** | **le** café | the coffee |
| **la** | **la** bière | the beer |
| **l'** | **l'**eau (f) | the water |
| **l'** | **l'**homme (m) | the man |
| **les** | **les** croissants | the croissants |

**Elision reminder:** When LE or LA comes before a vowel (a, e, i, o, u) or silent H, they become **l'** :
- le + homme → **l'**homme
- la + eau → **l'**eau
- la + école → **l'**école

## Observation 2: LE/LA/LES vs UN/UNE/DES — What's the Difference?

**Compare these sentences:**

| With UN/UNE/DES | With LE/LA/LES |
|:-----------------|:---------------|
| Je voudrais **un** café. (any coffee) | **Le** café est bon ici. (this specific coffee) |
| J'ai **une** voiture. (I have a car) | **La** voiture est rouge. (the specific car is red) |
| Il y a **des** croissants. (there are some) | **Les** croissants sont frais. (the specific ones) |

### The Rule

| Article | Meaning | Example |
|:--------|:--------|:--------|
| **UN/UNE/DES** | **a / some** — not specific | Je voudrais **un** café. (any coffee) |
| **LE/LA/LES** | **the** — specific | **Le** café est sur la table. (that specific coffee) |

**Think of it this way:**
- **UN** café = **a** coffee (you don't care which one)
- **LE** café = **the** coffee (a specific one, or coffee in general)

**Important:** LE/LA/LES are also used to talk about things **in general**:
- J'aime **le** chocolat. = I like chocolate (in general).
- **Les** Belges aiment **les** frites. = Belgians like fries (in general).

## Culture: Shopping in Belgium

### Useful to Know

**Opening hours in Belgium:**
- Most shops: 10h-18h (closed Sundays, except in tourist areas)
- Supermarkets: 8h-20h (some open until 21h)
- Bakeries: 7h-18h (open Sundays morning!)
- Night shops: 18h-1h (when everything else is closed!)

**Belgian grocery chains (you'll see these everywhere):**

| Chain | Type | Note |
|:------|:-----|:-----|
| Delhaize | Supermarket | Wide selection, moderate prices |
| Colruyt | Supermarket | Cheapest prices guaranteed |
| Carrefour | Supermarket | French chain, very common |
| Proxy / Shop & Go | Mini-market | Small Delhaize stores in the city |
| Aldi / Lidl | Discount | Budget-friendly |

**Tom, American expat:**
> "The first thing I learned in Belgium: always say 'Bonjour' when you enter a shop, and 'Au revoir' when you leave. And if you need something after 8pm, look for a 'night shop' — they're everywhere in Brussels!"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Using LE before a vowel

| Wrong | Correct |
|:------|:--------|
| **le** homme | **l'**homme |
| **la** eau | **l'**eau |

Before a vowel → **l'** (elision!)

### Mistake 2: Confusing UN and LE

| Wrong | Correct |
|:------|:--------|
| J'aime **un** chocolat. (I love chocolate in general) | J'aime **le** chocolat. |
| **Le** café, s'il vous plaît. (ordering any coffee) | **Un** café, s'il vous plaît. |

For general likes → LE/LA/LES. For ordering one item → UN/UNE.

### Mistake 3: Forgetting that LE/LA/LES show gender

| Wrong | Correct |
|:------|:--------|
| **le** boulangerie | **la** boulangerie (feminine!) |

LE = masculine, LA = feminine — just like UN = masculine, UNE = feminine.

## Grammar Summary

### Definite Articles: LE / LA / L' / LES

| | Masculine | Feminine | Before vowel | Plural |
|:--|:---------|:---------|:-------------|:-------|
| | **le** | **la** | **l'** | **les** |

### UN/UNE/DES vs LE/LA/LES

| | UN/UNE/DES (indefinite) | LE/LA/LES (definite) |
|:--|:------------------------|:---------------------|
| Meaning | a / some (not specific) | the (specific) |
| General statement | ✗ | J'aime **le** chocolat. |
| First mention | Il y a **un** parc. | ✗ |
| Specific | ✗ | **Le** parc est joli. |
| Ordering one | **Un** café, svp. | ✗ |
