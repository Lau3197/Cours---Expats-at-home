## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Before we dive into grammar, watch out for these common mistakes:**

**Mistake 1:** Forgetting to use the infinitive after "aimer": "J'aime lis" âŒ  
**Correct:** "J'aime lire" (I like to read) âœ…

**Mistake 2:** Using "Je aime" instead of "J'aime" âŒ  
**Correct:** "J'aime" (the "e" is dropped before a vowel) âœ…  
**Why:** When "je" is followed by a word starting with a vowel, drop the "e" and add an apostrophe: "J'aime" not "Je aime".

**Correct:** Use "J'aime lire" for "I like to read", "J'aime beaucoup lire" for "I like reading a lot", "J'adore lire" for "I love reading" âœ…  
**Why:** "Aimer" alone means "to like/love". "Aimer bien" is less common. Use "aimer", "aimer beaucoup", or "adorer" for different intensity levels.
