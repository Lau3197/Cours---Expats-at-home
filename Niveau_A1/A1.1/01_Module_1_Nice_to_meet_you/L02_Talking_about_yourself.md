## Observation 1: The Verb AVOIR (to have)

**Look at the dialogue:**

> **Yuki :** Vous **avez** quel âge ?
> **Tom :** **J'ai** trente-deux ans.
> **Yuki :** **J'ai** vingt-huit ans. Vous **avez** un numéro ?

**What do you notice?**

- "J'ai" = "I have" (elision: je + ai = **j'ai**)
- "Vous avez" = "You have"
- AVOIR is used for **age** in French: "J'ai 32 ans" (literally: I have 32 years)

### Full Conjugation of AVOIR

| French | Pronunciation | English |
|:-------|:-------------|:--------|
| j'**ai** | /ʒe/ | I have |
| tu **as** | /ty a/ | you have (informal) |
| il/elle **a** | /il a/ · /ɛl a/ | he/she has |
| nous **avons** | /nu za.vɔ̃/ | we have |
| vous **avez** | /vu za.ve/ | you have (formal/plural) |
| ils/elles **ont** | /il zɔ̃/ · /ɛl zɔ̃/ | they have |

**Remember: j'ai, not "je ai"!** This is elision (you learned this in Lesson 1).

## Observation 3: Numbers 20-100 — The Belgian Way!

### The Tens in Belgium

| Number | Belgium | France | Note |
|:-------|:--------|:-------|:-----|
| 20 | vingt | vingt | Same |
| 30 | trente | trente | Same |
| 40 | quarante | quarante | Same |
| 50 | cinquante | cinquante | Same |
| 60 | soixante | soixante | Same |
| 70 | **septante** | soixante-dix | **Different!** |
| 80 | quatre-vingts | quatre-vingts | Same |
| 90 | **nonante** | quatre-vingt-dix | **Different!** |
| 100 | cent | cent | Same |

**In Belgium, 70 = septante and 90 = nonante.** This is MUCH more logical than the French system! If you watch French TV, you'll hear "soixante-dix" and "quatre-vingt-dix" — but in Belgium (and Switzerland), we keep it simple.

### Combining: tens + units

| Number | French (Belgium) |
|:-------|:----------------|
| 21 | vingt-**et**-un |
| 22 | vingt-deux |
| 35 | trente-cinq |
| 47 | quarante-sept |
| 71 | **septante**-et-un |
| 78 | **septante**-huit |
| 93 | **nonante**-trois |

**Rule:** tens + hyphen + unit. Exception: 21, 31, 41... use "et" (vingt-**et**-un).

## Observation 4: Saying Your Age

**In French, you don't "be" an age — you "have" years!**

| French | Literal translation | English |
|:-------|:-------------------|:--------|
| J'ai 30 ans. | I have 30 years. | I am 30 years old. |
| Tu as quel âge ? | You have which age? | How old are you? |
| Il a 45 ans. | He has 45 years. | He is 45 years old. |

**Common mistake:** "Je **suis** 30 ans" is WRONG. Always use AVOIR for age!

### The Question: "Tu as quel âge ?" / "Vous avez quel âge ?"

| Informal | Formal |
|:---------|:-------|
| Tu as quel âge ? | Vous avez quel âge ? |

## Culture: Numbers in Belgium

### Septante and Nonante

Belgium and Switzerland use **septante** (70) and **nonante** (90). This is MORE logical than the French system:
- France: soixante-dix (60+10) = 70 ?!
- Belgium: **septante** = 70. Simple!

If a French person says "soixante-dix" → they mean 70. If a Belgian says "septante" → same number!

### Belgian Phone Numbers

Belgian phone numbers have 10 digits:
- Mobile: **04XX XX XX XX** (starting with 04)
- Brussels landline: **02 XXX XX XX**
- Other cities: **0X XXX XX XX**

### Useful: Your Numéro National

When you register at the commune, you'll get a national number (numéro de registre national). Format: **YY.MM.DD-XXX.XX**

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: "Je suis 30 ans" instead of "J'ai 30 ans"

| Wrong | Correct |
|:------|:--------|
| Je **suis** 30 ans. | J'**ai** 30 ans. |

In French, you "have" an age!

### Mistake 2: Forgetting "ans"

| Wrong | Correct |
|:------|:--------|
| J'ai 30. | J'ai 30 **ans**. |

Always add "ans" after the number.

### Mistake 3: Saying "soixante-dix" in Belgium

| France | Belgium |
|:-------|:--------|
| soixante-dix | **septante** |
| quatre-vingt-dix | **nonante** |

You're in Belgium — use septante and nonante!
