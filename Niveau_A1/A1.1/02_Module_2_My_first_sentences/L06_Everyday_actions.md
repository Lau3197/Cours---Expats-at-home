## Let's Start! A Day in Brussels

### Listen to this dialogue

**Sophie describes her typical day to Tom:**

> **Tom :** Qu'est-ce que tu fais dans une journée normale ?
>
> **Sophie :** Le matin, je **mange** un croissant et je **regarde** les nouvelles. Ensuite, je **travaille** de 9 heures à 17 heures.
>
> **Tom :** Tu **parles** français au travail ?
>
> **Sophie :** Oui, **on parle** français et néerlandais. C'est normal à Bruxelles ! À midi, **on mange** ensemble à la cafétéria.
>
> **Tom :** Et le soir ?
>
> **Sophie :** Le soir, j'**écoute** de la musique ou je **regarde** un film. Le weekend, **on visite** des musées ou **on marche** dans le parc.
>
> **Tom :** Les Belges **aiment** les musées ?
>
> **Sophie :** Oui, **on adore** les musées ! Et **on mange** des gaufres après !

<details>
<summary>English Translation</summary>

> **Tom:** What do you do on a normal day?
>
> **Sophie:** In the morning, I **eat** a croissant and I **watch** the news. Then, I **work** from 9 to 5.
>
> **Tom:** Do you **speak** French at work?
>
> **Sophie:** Yes, **we speak** French and Dutch. That's normal in Brussels! At noon, **we eat** together in the cafeteria.
>
> **Tom:** And in the evening?
>
> **Sophie:** In the evening, I **listen to** music or I **watch** a movie. On weekends, **we visit** museums or **we walk** in the park.
>
> **Tom:** Do Belgians **like** museums?
>
> **Sophie:** Yes, **we love** museums! And **we eat** waffles afterwards!

## Observation 1: Regular -ER Verbs — The Biggest Verb Family!

**Look at the dialogue. Find all the action words ending in -ER:**

> je **mange** — je **regarde** — je **travaille** — tu **parles** — on **mange** — j'**écoute** — on **visite** — on **marche**

**What do you notice?**

All these verbs end in **-ER** in the infinitive: manger, regarder, travailler, parler, écouter, visiter, marcher. These are **regular -ER verbs** — and they ALL follow the same pattern!

### The -ER Conjugation Pattern

**Take the verb PARLER (to speak):**

1. Remove the -ER ending → **parl-**
2. Add the correct ending:

| Subject | Ending | Conjugation | Pronunciation |
|:--------|:-------|:------------|:-------------|
| je | **-e** | je parl**e** | [parl] |
| tu | **-es** | tu parl**es** | [parl] |
| il/elle/on | **-e** | il parl**e** | [parl] |
| nous | **-ons** | nous parl**ons** | [par-LON] |
| vous | **-ez** | vous parl**ez** | [par-LAY] |
| ils/elles | **-ent** | ils parl**ent** | [parl] |

**Big secret:** Je, tu, il/elle/on, and ils/elles all sound THE SAME! Only the writing changes. The only forms that sound different are **nous** (-ons) and **vous** (-ez).

### Essential -ER Verbs

| French | English |
|:-------|:--------|
| parler | to speak |
| manger | to eat |

## Observation 2: ON — The Most Important Pronoun in Spoken French!

**Look at the dialogue:**

> **On parle** français et néerlandais.
> **On mange** ensemble à la cafétéria.
> **On visite** des musées.
> **On adore** les musées !

**What is "ON"?**

- **ON** = **we** in everyday spoken French
- Belgians and French people use "on" much more than "nous" in conversation
- "On" uses the **same conjugation as il/elle** (3rd person singular)

### ON = We (in spoken French)

| Formal/Written | Spoken/Everyday |
|:--------------|:---------------|
| **Nous** parlons français. | **On** parle français. |
| **Nous** mangeons à midi. | **On** mange à midi. |
| **Nous** habitons en Belgique. | **On** habite en Belgique. |

**ON is conjugated like IL/ELLE:** on parl**e**, on mang**e**, on habite, on travaille...

**In Belgium, you'll hear "on" much more than "nous" in daily life!**

> **Sophie says:** "En Belgique, **on** parle français, néerlandais et allemand. Trois langues !"

## Observation 3: Verbs + LE/LA/LES (Direct Objects)

**Look at these sentences from the dialogue:**

> Je **regarde les** nouvelles. (I watch **the** news)
> **On visite des** musées. (We visit museums)
> Les Belges **aiment les** musées. (Belgians love museums)

**What do you notice?**

After many verbs, you need an article before the noun:

| French | English |
|:-------|:--------|
| Je regarde **la** télé. | I watch TV. |
| J'écoute **la** radio. | I listen to the radio. |
| J'aime **le** chocolat. | I like chocolate. |
| Je mange **une** gaufre. | I eat a waffle. |
| On visite **les** musées. | We visit the museums. |

**Remember from Lesson 5:**
- **LE/LA/LES** for general things: "J'aime **le** chocolat." (chocolate in general)
- **UN/UNE/DES** for non-specific things: "Je mange **un** croissant." (one croissant)

## Culture: Languages in Belgium

### Did You Know?

Belgium has **3 official languages**:
- **French** (spoken in Wallonia and Brussels)
- **Dutch/Flemish** (spoken in Flanders and Brussels)
- **German** (spoken in a small area in the east)

**Brussels is bilingual** (French-Dutch), but in practice, most people speak French. Many also speak English, especially in the EU quarter.

**Sophie, teacher in Brussels:**
> "À Bruxelles, on parle français, néerlandais et souvent anglais aussi. Dans mon école, les élèves parlent parfois 4 ou 5 langues ! C'est normal ici."

> "In Brussels, we speak French, Dutch, and often English too. In my school, students sometimes speak 4 or 5 languages! That's normal here."

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Forgetting that -ent is SILENT

| Writing | Pronunciation |
|:--------|:-------------|
| Ils parl**ent** | [parl] — NOT [parlent]! |
| Elles mang**ent** | [manj] — the -ent is silent! |

### Mistake 2: Using "nous" when "on" is more natural

| Textbook French | Real spoken French |
|:---------------|:------------------|
| Nous mangeons | **On mange** |
| Nous habitons | **On habite** |

In daily life, Belgians say "on" 95% of the time!

### Mistake 3: Conjugating ON like NOUS

| Wrong | Correct |
|:------|:--------|
| On **parlons** | On **parle** |
| On **mangeons** | On **mange** |

ON always uses the il/elle conjugation!

## Grammar Summary

### -ER Verb Conjugation

| Subject | Ending | Example (parler) |
|:--------|:-------|:----------------|
| je | -e | je parle |
| tu | -es | tu parles |
| il/elle/on | -e | il parle / on parle |
| nous | -ons | nous parlons |
| vous | -ez | vous parlez |
| ils/elles | -ent | ils parlent |

### ON = We (spoken French)

- On **parle** = Nous parlons
- ON uses the il/elle form (3rd person singular)
- Used 95% of the time in spoken French instead of "nous"
