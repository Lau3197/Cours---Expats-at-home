## Let's Start! Dinner in Brussels

### Listen to this dialogue

**Tom and Sophie have souper at a restaurant in Brussels:**

> **Serveur :** Bonsoir ! Vous avez réservé ?
>
> **Sophie :** Oui, une table pour deux au nom de Sophie.
>
> **Serveur :** Très bien. Suivez-moi. Voici le menu.
>
> *(quelques minutes plus tard)*
>
> **Serveur :** Vous avez choisi ?
>
> **Sophie :** Oui. **Je voudrais** la soupe à l'oignon en entrée.
>
> **Tom :** Et moi, **je prends** la salade César.
>
> **Serveur :** Et comme plat principal ?
>
> **Sophie :** **Pour moi**, le steak-frites, saignant s'il vous plaît.
>
> **Tom :** Et **pour moi**, les moules-frites.
>
> **Serveur :** Parfait. Et comme boisson ?
>
> **Tom :** **On boit** quoi, Sophie ? Une bouteille d'eau et deux bières ?
>
> **Sophie :** Oui ! Deux Chimay, s'il vous plaît.
>
> **Serveur :** Très bien. Je vous apporte ça tout de suite.

<details>
<summary>English Translation</summary>

> **Waiter:** Good evening! Do you have a reservation?
>
> **Sophie:** Yes, a table for two under Sophie.
>
> **Waiter:** Very well. Follow me. Here is the menu.
>
> *(a few minutes later)*
>
> **Waiter:** Have you chosen?
>
> **Sophie:** Yes. **I would like** the onion soup as a starter.
>
> **Tom:** And for me, **I'll have** the Caesar salad.
>
> **Waiter:** And for the main course?
>
> **Sophie:** **For me**, the steak and fries, rare please.
>
> **Tom:** And **for me**, the mussels and fries.
>
> **Waiter:** Perfect. And to drink?
>
> **Tom:** **What are we drinking**, Sophie? A bottle of water and two beers?
>
> **Sophie:** Yes! Two Chimays, please.
>
> **Waiter:** Very well. I'll bring that right away.

</details>

### Comprehension

1. Did Sophie make a reservation?
2. What does Sophie order as a starter?
3. How does Sophie like her steak?
4. What does Tom order?
5. What do they drink?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **Yes**, table for two — 2. Onion **soup** — 3. **Rare** (saignant) — 4. **Moules-frites** — 5. Water and two **Chimay** beers

## Observation 1: Ordering Expressions

### Ways to Order

| French | English | Politeness |
|:-------|:--------|:-----------|
| **Je voudrais**... | I would like... | Very polite ✓ |
| **J'aimerais**... | I would like... | Very polite ✓ |
| **Je prends**... | I'll have... | Standard |
| **Pour moi**... | For me... | Casual |

**Never say "Je veux" (I want) at a restaurant — it's too direct!**

## Observation 2: The Verb PRENDRE (to take / to have)

### Full Conjugation

| Subject | Conjugation |
|:--------|:------------|
| je | **prends** |
| tu | **prends** |
| il/elle/on | **prend** |
| nous | **prenons** |
| vous | **prenez** |
| ils/elles | **prennent** |

### Usage at a Restaurant

| French | English |
|:-------|:--------|
| Je prends le menu du jour. | I'll have the daily special. |
| Qu'est-ce que vous prenez ? | What are you having? |
| On prend une bouteille de vin. | We'll have a bottle of wine. |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Why? |
|:------------|:----------|:-----|
| ~~Je **veux** une bière.~~ | **Je voudrais** une bière. | "Je veux" is too direct at a restaurant |
| ~~Ils **prendent** le menu.~~ | Ils **prennent** le menu. | PRENDRE doubles the N in ils/elles |
| ~~Nous **boivons** du vin.~~ | Nous **buvons** du vin. | The stem changes to BUV- for nous/vous |
| ~~Le **dîner** = dinner~~ | Le **dîner** = **lunch** (in Belgium!) | Belgian ≠ France meal names |
| ~~Je prends **le** steak saignant.~~ | ✅ Both OK | "Le" or no article — both acceptable |

## Culture: Dining in Belgium

### Important — Belgian Meal Names!

| Belgian French | France French | English | Time |
|:--------------|:-------------|:--------|:-----|
| le **déjeuner** | le petit-déjeuner | breakfast | morning |
| le **dîner** | le déjeuner | lunch | noon |
| le **souper** | le dîner | dinner | evening |

**This is one of the biggest differences with France!** In Belgium, "dîner" = lunch (noon), NOT dinner. "Souper" = dinner (evening).

### Belgian Restaurant Customs

- **Tipping:** Service IS included. But you can round up or leave 5-10% for great service.
- **Water:** NOT free. You pay for mineral water (plate = still, pétillante = sparkling).
- **"S'il vous plaît"** is used at the END of an order in Belgium — like a period: "Une bière, **s'il vous plaît**."
- **Bancontact** is the standard Belgian debit card system — "Je paye par Bancontact."
- **Pace:** Meals in Belgium are long (2-3 hours). It's social!

### Belgian Specialties

| Dish | Description |
|:-----|:------------|
| Moules-frites | Mussels with fries — the national dish! |
| Carbonade flamande | Beef stewed in beer |
| Waterzooi | Creamy chicken or fish stew |
| Vol-au-vent | Chicken in creamy sauce, in puff pastry |
| Stoemp | Mashed potatoes with vegetables |
