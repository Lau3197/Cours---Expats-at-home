## Let's Start! At a chocolate shop

### Listen to this dialogue

**Sophie takes Tom to a famous chocolate shop:**

> **Vendeur :** Bonjour ! Bienvenue chez Neuhaus. Je peux vous aider ?
> 
> **Sophie :** Bonjour ! On voudrait goûter quelques chocolats.
> 
> **Vendeur :** Bien sûr ! Nous avons des pralines au praliné, au caramel, et au chocolat noir.
> 
> **Tom :** C'est quoi exactement une praline ?
> 
> **Vendeur :** C'est une spécialité belge – un chocolat fourré inventé ici à Bruxelles en 1912 !
> 
> **Sophie :** Je voudrais goûter celle au caramel.
> 
> **Tom :** Et moi, celle au chocolat noir.
> 
> **Vendeur :** Voilà ! Alors, qu'est-ce que vous en pensez ?
> 
> **Tom :** Mmm, c'est fondant et pas trop sucré. Délicieux !
> 
> **Sophie :** Le caramel est crémeux et doux. J'adore !

---

### Comprehension

**Answer the questions:**

1. What shop do they visit?
2. What is a praline?
3. When was it invented?
4. What flavor does Sophie choose?
5. How does Tom describe his chocolate?

<summary>Answers</summary>

1. Neuhaus chocolate shop
2. A filled chocolate invented in Brussels
3. 1912
4. Caramel
5. Melting and not too sweet

## Observation 1: Belgian food vocabulary

### Chocolate

| French | English |
|:-------|:--------|
| le chocolat | chocolate |

| le chocolat noir | dark chocolate |
| le chocolat au lait | milk chocolate |
| le chocolat blanc | white chocolate |

### Waffles

| French | English |
|:-------|:--------|
| une gaufre | waffle |
| une gaufre de Bruxelles | Brussels waffle (light, rectangular) |
| une gaufre de Liège | Liège waffle (dense, sweet, irregular) |

| avec du chocolat | with chocolate |

## Culture: Belgian culinary heritage

### Read this testimony

**Jean-Pierre, Belgian chocolatier:**

> "Belgian chocolate is famous worldwide. We invented the praline in 1912, and the technique of using 100% cocoa butter. There are over 2,000 chocolate shops in Belgium! Each region also has its waffle tradition: Brussels waffles are light and crispy, usually eaten with toppings. Liège waffles are denser and sweeter, perfect for eating on the go."

### Famous Belgian food

| Specialty | Description |
|:----------|:------------|
| Pralines | Filled chocolates, invented 1912 |
| Gaufre de Bruxelles | Light, rectangular, with toppings |
| Gaufre de Liège | Dense, sweet, caramelized sugar |
| Speculoos | Spiced biscuit |
| Cuberdon | Raspberry-flavored candy from Ghent |
| Pistolet | Belgian bread roll |

### Famous chocolate brands

| Brand | Since | Known for |
|:------|:------|:----------|
| Neuhaus | 1857 | Invented the praline |
| Leonidas | 1913 | Affordable luxury |
| Godiva | 1926 | International fame |
| Pierre Marcolini | 1995 | Artisanal, bean-to-bar |
| Mary | 1919 | Royal warrant |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Confusing waffle types

| Gaufre de Bruxelles | Gaufre de Liège |
|:--------------------|:----------------|
| Light, crispy | Dense, sweet |
| Rectangular | Irregular shape |
| Needs toppings | Sweet on its own |

### Mistake 2: Wrong gender

| Incorrect | Correct |
|:----------|:--------|
| un gaufre | **une** gaufre |
| un praline | **une** praline |

### Mistake 3: "Chocolat chaud" vs "chocolat"

| French | Meaning |
|:-------|:--------|
| un chocolat | a piece of chocolate |
| un chocolat chaud | hot chocolate (drink) |
